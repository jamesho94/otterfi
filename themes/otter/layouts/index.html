Personal Finance 2.0

Our approach to personal finance revolves around the simple premise that managing your money like a business will improve your financial situation without radically changing your lifestyle.

This blog will provide you with a framework to objectively evaluate your finances and monitor changes over time. You'll learn corporate finance and accounting principles that will allow you to optimize your spending, maximize your savings, and increase your personal profitability.

Best of all, everything on this site is 100% free.

About Us

We’re professional accountants with a passion for personal finance (and otters). We’ve learned so much from the personal finance community and this blog is just our way of paying it forward.

We hope that you’ll benefit from the information that we provide and if you have any questions, don’t hesitate to reach out.


Profit & Loss


Objectively evaluate your financial situation through personal financial statements that will monitor your spending and calculate your net worth. Learn how to use financial metrics to gain insights and find areas of improvement.

Budgeting


Ensure your spending matches your goals and priorities by developing a realistic and comprehensive budget that will help you stay accountable without feeling deprived.

Debt & Credit


Learn debt management strategies that will minimize your interest payments while accelerating your payoff date. Understand how your credit score is calculated and how to protect your good standing.

Insurance


Prevent inconveniences from becoming catastrophes through insurance products that manage your appropriate level of risk. Learn the various types of coverage and what limits should be set on your policies.

Investments


Learn how to set the proper asset allocation in a diversified portfolio and enjoy the benefits of compound interest on your money. Maximize your returns by finding low cost providers and self-managing your investments.

Retirement


Discover the benefits of financial independence so that you can retire on your own terms. Learn how to calculate your target nest egg and determine a safe withdrawal rate so that you can enjoy the most valuable resource: time.
